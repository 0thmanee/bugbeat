FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Clearing Up Doubts

How does Bugbeat handles vulnerabilities?

Bugbeat emphasizes responsible disclosure by providing a secure platform for hackers to report vulnerabilities. Upon receiving a report, Bugbeat's team verifies and communicates the findings to the affected company, allowing them ample time to address the vulnerabilities before public disclosure.

Are the programs on Bugbeat open to anyone?

Yes, Bugbeat welcomes security researchers of all skill levels to participate in bug bounty programs. However, specific programs may have additional requirements or eligibility criteria mentioned in their program guidelines.

How are rewards determined for submissions?

Bugbeat follows a reward framework that takes into account the severity, impact, and novelty of the reported vulnerabilities. Rewards are assigned based on these factors, with higher rewards for more critical vulnerabilities.

How long does it take for triage and addresse?

Bugbeat's triage process aims to be efficient. Upon submission, vulnerabilities undergo a thorough assessment and are prioritized based on severity. The timeframe for resolution varies depending on the complexity of the issues and the company's internal processes, but Bugbeat encourages timely responses from companies.

Can I submit vulnerabilities anonymously?

Bugbeat allows you to submit vulnerabilities anonymously if desired. However, providing contact details enables smoother collaboration with the affected company for addressing and resolving the reported vulnerabilities.

How can companies benefit from BugBeat?

Bugbeat offers companies an opportunity to leverage the skills of ethical hackers to uncover vulnerabilities before they can be exploited maliciously. Engaging with Bugbeat's community helps companies identify and address security weaknesses, leading to improved overall security and customer trust.
